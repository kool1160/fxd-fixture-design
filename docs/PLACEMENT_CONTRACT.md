# FXD Placement Contract

Milestone 22 adds a deterministic proof-layer placement engine for datum candidates, rests, stops, round and diamond pins, supports, and clamps.

Callers provide the geometry-aware evidence that the neutral product model cannot invent: stable geometry references, positions, normals, surface area, accessibility, stability, critical-characteristic relationship, distortion sensitivity, assumptions, and confidence. Candidate ranking is deterministic. Invalid references, missing evidence, unsupported mounts, collisions, insufficient constraint rank, duplicated constraint directions, blocked access, and inadequate clamp capacity fail closed or remain explicit warnings.

The placement engine composes the existing six-DOF constraint solver, access analysis, vendor-neutral tooling library, weld annotations, and Milestone 21 structural members. Supports and clamps are recorded for load transfer and reaction review but do not silently become locating constraints. Standard tooling is selected before custom geometry when the supplied metadata is adequate. Alternative datum arrangements are retained for review when multiple eligible candidates exist.

Placement output is editable, source-traceable, millimetre-based, deterministically serialized, and digestible as validation evidence. It is not final B-Rep geometry, robot motion planning, a force certificate, thermal simulation, safety certification, or production approval. Qualified fixture-engineering review remains mandatory. Future work can add kernel-derived face normals and area, richer weld and spatter exclusion geometry, approach envelopes, structural mount optimization, and manufacturing-ready components without changing the placement contract.
